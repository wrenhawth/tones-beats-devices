# Terms

## Important Terms

### [MIDI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MIDI)

A standard for capturing musical information in a digital format. At a basic level, it captures the time, pitch, duration of notes that are played, as well as additional messages that can be used to control various aspects of synthesizers, music software, or other musical devices.

### [Chord](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chord_(music)>)

A group of three or more notes. A chord has a **root note** (the note which the chord is built on), and a **quality** (the type of chord).

For example, the chord _C major_ has a root note of C and a quality of major.

## Music Software Categories

### [Virtual Instrument](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_synthesizer)

A broad term for an app or plug-in that generates music from some sort of input. This input can be a device such as a MIDI keyboard or drum pad (referred to as [MIDI controllers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MIDI_controller)), or through touch, keyboard, or some other sort of interface.

Can often be either played lived or recorded (often through recording the [MIDI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MIDI) input)

### [Sequencer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Music_sequencer)

Focused on constructing beats, melodies, and/or chord progressions. Usually contains some type of grid that loops automatically. (Example from [Logic Pro](https://support.apple.com/en-us/102117))

> Example: [Step Sequencer from Logic Pro](https://support.apple.com/en-us/102117)
> ![sequencer](https://cdsassets.apple.com/live/7WUAS350/images/pro-apps/logic/logic-pro-x-step-sequencer.jpg)

### [Sampler](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sampler_(musical_instrument)>)

Focused on recording and playing back short pieces of music called samples. Typically involves some sort of sequencer or other form of musical input. Will sometimes allow users to edit the sampler (e.g. take a single sound and then allow the user to play it in different pitches). (Example: [Koala Sampler](https://www.koalasampler.com/))
